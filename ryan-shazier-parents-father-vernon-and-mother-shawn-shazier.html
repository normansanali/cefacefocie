    Ryan Shazier, the former football player, was born to his parents Vernon and Shawn on September 6, 1992.

    His career in the NFL was ended by a tragic event that led to his retirement after four years in football.

    Throughout this difficult journey, his parents remained a constant source of support despite the challenges he faced in his career.

    Ryan Shazier gained recognition as a linebacker for the Pittsburgh Steelers in the NFL.

    His career began at Ohio State University, later joining the Steelers after being drafted in 2014.

    In a game against the Cincinnati Bengals in 2017, he suffered a significant injury from a head-first tackle, leading to paralysis.

    After undergoing spinal stabilization surgery and intense rehabilitation, he spent two seasons on the Steelers’ physically-unable-to-perform list before retiring in 2020.

    Post-retirement, Shazier has dedicated himself to community work, establishing a nonprofit that supports individuals dealing with specific disabilities.
