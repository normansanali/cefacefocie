    Biography

    Aaron Douglas was born in Topeka, KS on May 26, 1899. The Painter and illustrator used to be associated with the Harlem Renaissance and known for “Power Plant, Harlem” and other works. He was called the Father of African-American Arts. He and Langston Hughes were both well-known figures of the Harlem Renaissance. He graduated from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln and moved to New York City three years later. His illustrations adorned works by James Weldon Johnson and Countee Cullen.
