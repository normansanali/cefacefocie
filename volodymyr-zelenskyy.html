    Volodymyr Zelenskyy Biography

    Volodymyr Oleksandrovych Zelenskyy was born in Kryvyi Rih, then known as the Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic, on January 25, 1978, to Jewish parents. Oleksandr Zelenskyy is a professor and the chairman of the Department of Cybernetics and Computing Hardware at the Kryvyi Rih State University of Economics and Technology; his mother, Rymma Zelenska, previously worked as an engineer. Semyon (Simon) Ivanovych Zelenskyy, Semyon’s grandpa, participated in the Red Army (57th Guards Motor Rifle Division) during World War II; Semyon’s father and three brothers were murdered during the Holocaust.

    Before beginning elementary school, Zelenskyy spent four years in Erdenet, Mongolia, where his father worked. He passed the Test of English as a Foreign Language at 16 and was awarded an education grant to study in Israel, but his father refused to let him go. Moreover, he eventually got a law degree at the Kryvyi Rih Institute of Economics, previously a department of Kyiv National Economic University. He is now a part of Kryvyi Rih National University but did not pursue a career in law.

    Career

    Volodymyr Zelenskyy is a politician from Ukraine, a former actor, and a humorist. Since 2019, he has served as Ukraine’s president. In the early stages of his career, he pursued comedy. He founded the production company Kvartal 95, which creates films, cartoons, and television shows, including Servant of the People (2015–2019), in which Zelenskyy played the role of Ukraine’s president. In March 2018, Kvartal 95 personnel launched a political and social gathering with the same name as the television show. On December 31, 2018, he announced his candidacy for the 2019 Ukrainian presidential election.

    He defeated Poroshenko with 73.2 percent of the vote in the second round. He has positioned himself as an anti-establishment, anti-corruption populist. Furthermore, he supervised the lifting of authorized immunity for members of Ukraine’s parliament, the nation’s response to the COVID-19 outbreak and accompanying financial collapse, and some success in combating corruption during his government.

    Additionally, as part of his presidential campaign, he promised to end Ukraine’s protracted conflict with Russia and attempted to engage in conversation with Russian President Vladimir Putin. In February 2022, his government faced an escalation of tensions with Russia, culminating at the beginning of a continuing full-scale Russian invasion. Following the invasion’s conclusion, he imposed martial law throughout Ukraine and routine mobilization.

    Ukraine’s President: ‘Russia is attacking schools, ambulances, and everything.’

    Volodymyr Zelensky reported Sunday (February 17, 2022) that Moscow was bombarding residential areas in Ukraine as its invading forces attempted to penetrate deeper into the pro-Western country. “The previous night in Ukraine was horrible, with yet another round of shooting and bombardment of residential areas and civilian infrastructure,” Zelensky remarked in an online interview. “At the moment, there is no object in the country that the occupants do not regard as an appropriate target. They oppose everyone. They wage war on all life forms – against kindergartens, residential structures, and even ambulances. He stated that Russian forces were “shooting rockets and missiles at entire city neighborhoods where there is no military infrastructure and never has been.”
