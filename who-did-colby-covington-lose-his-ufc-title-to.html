    Colby Covington, a former interim champion in the UFC welterweight division, had a highly anticipated rematch with Kamaru Usman for the UFC Welterweight Championship on November 6, 2021, at UFC 268. Despite putting up a tough fight, Covington lost the bout via unanimous decision, relinquishing his title aspirations. In this article, we will delve into the details of Covington's loss to Usman and explore the current landscape of the welterweight division.

    The Rematch with Kamaru Usman

    Colby Covington's rematch with Kamaru Usman was eagerly awaited by fans and pundits alike. The fight took place at UFC 268, and Covington put up a valiant effort against the reigning champion. However, despite a close bout, Covington was unable to secure victory, ultimately losing via unanimous decision. This defeat marked a setback in Covington's quest for the welterweight title.

    Post-Fight Analysis

    Following his loss to Usman, Colby Covington's record stood at 2-2 in his last four fights. Despite this recent inconsistency, Covington still maintains his position as the top contender for Leon Edwards' welterweight belt. Edwards, the current champion, defeated Usman in 2022 to claim the title and now views Covington as no match for him. Covington's previous achievements as an interim champion and his overall skill set have solidified his status as a formidable challenger in the division.

    UFC 296: Edwards vs. Covington

    The upcoming main event of UFC 296 will feature a welterweight title fight between Leon Edwards and Colby Covington. Edwards, the reigning champion, will defend his belt against Covington, who is determined to bring home the undisputed title. Despite his recent losses, Covington remains the top contender for Edwards' belt due to his impressive track record and undeniable potential.

    Co-Main Event and Other Exciting Fights

    In addition to the Edwards vs. Covington title fight, UFC 296 will also showcase a co-main event rematch for the flyweight title between Alexandre Pantoja and Brandon Royval. Pantoja, the current champion, boasts a four-fight win streak and will look to continue his dominance in the division. The main card of the event will also feature a welterweight clash between Shavkat Rakhmonov and Stephen Thompson, as well as a lightweight bout between Tony Ferguson and Paddy Pimblett. These exciting matchups will add to the overall excitement and anticipation surrounding UFC 296.

    Colby Covington lost his UFC title to Kamaru Usman in a closely contested rematch at UFC 268. Despite this setback, Covington remains the top contender for Leon Edwards' welterweight belt. Covington's upcoming fight with Edwards at UFC 296 will provide him with an opportunity to reclaim championship glory. With a stacked card featuring other intriguing fights, UFC 296 promises to be an exciting event for MMA fans worldwide.
