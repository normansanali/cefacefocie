    Wendi Nix Biography

    Wendi Nix is an American anchor and sports reporter working as the host of ESPN’s NFL Live. She became the primary host of the company’s year-round weekday pro football studio show in February 2018.

    Nix is the new Number One show co-host of ESPN, NFL Live. Nix primarily manages NFL coverage and college football games involving New England area teams. Nix was a weekend sports anchor at WHDH in Boston (2003–2006) before working for ESPN, and also worked for Fox Sports Net, NESN and WPDE in South Carolina. Nix remains an occasional guest analyst on WHDH’s “Football Extra” weekend show.

Nix was president of the student body at Wofford and captain of the golf team.

    Wendi Nix Career

    Nix’s broadcasting career began in 1999 with ABC affiliate WPDE-TV in Myrtle Beach-Florence, S.C., where, in addition to reporting, she covered NASCAR and high school sports. In addition to hosting NFL Live, Nix was a co-host for three seasons (2014-16) on Sunday NFL Countdown with Chris Berman.

    She also presented weekly Chalk Talk gatherings in Monday Night Football cities in 2009, timed to MNF prime time games for ESPN. In August 2006 Nix joined ESPN as a reporter and host covering college soccer, NFL, and golf. Beyond NFL, Nix broadcast Saturdays on ESPN2 College Football Live and college football. She has since contributed to both SportsCenter and GameDay School. Her golf history includes three of the four big players-the the U.S. The Masters and the Open Championship, Open (2007-10).
